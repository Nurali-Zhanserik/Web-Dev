Between the lines

I chase the quiet between each thought,
Where doubts dissolve and fears are caught.
The world grows still, the noise turns thin,
And something brighter hums within.

I stumble, sure—but still I stand,
With open eyes and shaking hand.
Each step I take, though small, is mine,
A crooked path toward a distant shine.

If storms arrive and shadows stay,
I’ll learn their language, not run away.
For even night, when held just right,
Is only practice for the light.
